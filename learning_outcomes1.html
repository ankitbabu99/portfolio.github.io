- Collaboration

Any number of examples arise from New Tech that precisely display collaboration, however the most unique example is also the most outlandish. The Mr. President project preformed this year involved 3/4 of the school, changed where most students would customarily go to class, and addressed a single goal. The task was to single out one problem with the American education system and create a solution. This problem could have been anything, but it was for the student groups to decide. Examples and facts of this problem also had to be presented in an ordered manner, however the strangest groups were formed. The average group consisted of one senior, one junior, and one freshman. Working with different grades on the same project was something never conducted before and proved to be an excellent change from the regular. Each member had far different levels of skill and had different assignments to compensate. We had to work as a team knowing each other was a grade higher or lower than we were. The following work was a leter assignment thatl pertains to the project, the topic the letter covers was formed with the groups attention. This type of collaboration stands out from others given the grade placement and how action was taken.

- Critical Thinking

Nearly every project at New Tech involved this deep level of understanding and thought as well as it's utilization to the task. The WW2 book project in sophomore year required the most focus and direction. The project was rather large for 10th graders and looking back on this assignment it doesn’t pose much of a challenge now. Never the less, groups of two were required to make an illustrated story based off of a reading we were consigned. Each member had a specific role, either a writer or an illustrator. Being particularly absorbed in art this year and eagerly waiting a project that called for artistic skill, the illustrator role drastically spoke to me. I was teamed with an exceptional writer, Nektaria Baker who handled all the inscription portions. Our illustrated story “Bread and Ashes” was based off of Hassle and Gretel. It took massive amounts of focus to translate the writing and story into illustrations. There were 8 illustrations in total, however each had 1 or 2 drafts before the final piece was created. The amount of drawling was equitant to the amount of reading, turning words on pages into lines, landscapes, faces, and bodies. Translation of mood also played a major role how things were exemplified from text to image. Overall the project was very creative and interesting yet still it took much time and patients, high expectations for sophomores.

- Oral Communication

The most exciting project, out of 4 years of high school education was by far the Great Gatsby project in American studies, the junior humanities class. The point was to create a scene from the plot of the Great Gatsby and then perform live. Acting teams were formed and roles were taken based on the characters from the novel. It was important that actors made historical references in their parts and portrayed their characters accurately. I played Gatsby and spend hours working on his attitude, character, style, and emotion from the story. My communication score was 48/50 and received the Most Dramatic Death Award. Acting and improve are hobbies and having a project that focused on them while following a plot from F Scott Fitzgerald defiantly proved to be the most outstanding project.

- Written Communication

The number of large writing assignments and essays encountered at New Tech is overwhelming. One of the most exceptional assignments was the CRBIBS Real-Estate project in Political Studies. The end mission was to create a list of recommendations, investments, and buying strategies for a single consigned family. The group spend days looking for the best deal for our family and put massive amounts of concentration making sure the houses fit their needs and wants. Our written communication grade received an advanced evaluation. The challenge this project posed was assembling all the writing and then combining the work of four people together. The writing had to be the upmost professional and the same type one would find reading an authentic housing recommendation. Overall the team did well and was a major written communication victory.
